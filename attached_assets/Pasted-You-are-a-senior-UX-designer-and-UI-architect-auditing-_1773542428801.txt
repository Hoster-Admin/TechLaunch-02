You are a senior UX designer and UI architect auditing the admin panel for TLMENA (tlmena.com) — a Product Hunt-style platform for the MENA/Arab world.

CONTEXT

Admin panel roles: Admin, Moderator, Editor
Stack: React, DM Sans + DM Mono fonts
Market: MENA/GCC, bilingual Arabic + English, RTL support required
Core workflows: product moderation, user management, entity management, community moderation, platform settings


AUDIT THESE AREAS:
1. Dashboard

Does it show the right information immediately on login?
Are stat cards clickable and linked to the relevant section?
Is there a shortcut to the Audit Log?
Are pending items (products awaiting approval) prominently surfaced?
What would an admin look for first — is it visible without scrolling?

2. Navigation & Sidebar

Is grouping logical for the three role types?
Are the most-used actions reachable in ≤2 clicks?
Is the active page state obvious?
Do moderators and editors see menu items they have no access to?
Are any important pages buried or hard to discover?

3. Product Moderation Flow

Is the approve/reject/feature flow clear and fast?
Can a moderator review a product and take action without leaving the page?
Is there enough product detail visible to make a decision?
Are bulk actions intuitive — select, action, confirm?
Is feedback given immediately after an action?

4. User Management

Is it easy to find a specific user quickly?
Are suspend/warn/delete actions clearly separated to prevent mistakes?
Is the user's activity history visible from the management screen?
Are destructive actions (delete) protected by confirmation?

5. Tables & Data Lists

Are columns prioritized correctly — most important info first?
Is sorting and filtering intuitive and fast?
Are empty states handled with helpful messaging?
Are long strings (names, emails, URLs) truncated gracefully without losing meaning?
Is pagination clear — does the user know where they are in the list?

6. Forms & Inputs

Are labels clear and unambiguous?
Are validation errors inline and specific?
Are required fields marked consistently?
Is there confirmation before any destructive or irreversible action?
Are success and failure states visually distinct?

7. Modals & Dialogs

Can they be dismissed with ESC and backdrop click?
Is the primary action always visually dominant?
Are they the right size for their content — not too large or too small?
Are warning/destructive modals visually differentiated from standard ones?

8. Role-Based UI

Do moderators see admin-only actions they cannot perform?
Are restricted actions hidden or just disabled — and is the choice consistent?
Is the logged-in user's role visible somewhere in the UI at all times?
Are there any actions a role can trigger in the UI that the backend will reject?

9. Feedback & Loading States

Are there loading skeletons or spinners on every data fetch?
Do actions give immediate visual feedback before the server responds?
Are toast/error messages specific enough to tell the user what went wrong?
What happens if an action fails — is there a retry option?

10. RTL & Bilingual Readiness

Are there hardcoded LTR assumptions in padding, margin, flex direction, or icon placement?
Is Noto Kufi Arabic applied correctly to all Arabic strings?
Are Arabic strings truncated correctly in tables and cards?
Does the layout flip correctly when switching to Arabic?
Are numbers and dates formatted correctly for an Arabic-language context?

11. Consistency

Are spacing, color, and typography consistent across all pages?
Are button variants (primary, danger, ghost) used consistently?
Are icons from a single coherent set with consistent sizing?
Do similar actions always look and behave the same way across different pages?

12. Mobile & Responsiveness

Is the panel usable on a tablet (common in the GCC market)?
Does the sidebar collapse and expand correctly?
Are touch targets large enough for finger interaction?
Do tables degrade gracefully on smaller screens?
