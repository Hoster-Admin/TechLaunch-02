You are conducting a thorough UI/UX review of the TLMENA admin panel frontend. Analyze every screen, component, and user flow.

CONTEXT

Admin panel for a Product Hunt-style platform targeting the MENA/Arab market
Users: Admin, Moderator, Editor roles
Stack: React, pages/ and components/ folders
Fonts: DM Sans, DM Mono
Current known issues: no optimistic UI, no real-time updates, Audit Log not linked from Dashboard


REVIEW THESE AREAS:
1. Navigation & Information Architecture

Is the sidebar grouping logical?
Are the most-used actions reachable in ≤2 clicks?
Any orphaned pages with no clear entry point?
Does the active state clearly show where the user is?

2. Dashboard

Does it surface the right information at a glance?
Are stat cards actionable or just decorative?
What's missing that an admin would want to see first thing?

3. Tables & Data Lists

Are columns the right width and priority?
Is sorting and filtering intuitive?
Are empty states handled?
Are long strings (names, emails, URLs) truncated gracefully?

4. Forms & Input

Are error states clear and inline?
Are required fields marked?
Is there confirmation before destructive actions?
Are success/failure feedback messages consistent?

5. Modals & Dialogs

Are they dismissible in expected ways (ESC, backdrop click)?
Is the primary action always visually dominant?
Are they appropriately sized for their content?

6. Role-Based UI

Do moderators and editors see actions they can't perform?
Are disabled/restricted actions hidden or just greyed out?
Is the role clearly visible somewhere in the UI?

7. Feedback & Loading States

Are there loading skeletons or spinners on data fetches?
Do actions give immediate feedback before the server responds?
Are error toasts specific enough to be actionable?

8. RTL & Bilingual Readiness

Is the layout RTL-compatible for Arabic?
Are there any hardcoded LTR assumptions (padding, flex direction, icons)?
Are Arabic strings handled gracefully in tables and truncation?

9. Mobile & Responsiveness

Is the panel usable on tablet?
Does the sidebar collapse correctly?
Are touch targets large enough?

10. Consistency

Are spacing, color, and typography consistent across all pages?
Are button styles and variants used consistently?
Are icons from a single coherent set?


OUTPUT FORMAT
For each area:

✅ What works well
🔴 Critical issues (broken or confusing)
🟡 Improvements (friction or missing polish)
Specific fix recommendation for each issue found

End with:

Overall UX score out of 100
Top 5 highest-impact fixes ranked by effort vs improvement
